about me

I conduct interdisciplinary research at the intersection of machine learning, cognitive neuroscience, and psychology. My goal is to develop bio-inspired artificial intelligence systems that explain biological neural function and dysfunction. Before joining the University of Queenslandâ€™s Brain Institute as a Discovery Early Career Researcher in 2021, I worked at the University of Cambridge as a postdoctoral researcher from 2015 with Andrew Welchman, where I was awarded a Leverhulme Early Career Fellowship and an Isaac Newton Fellowship. I completed my PhD and Honours with Mark Edwards at the Australian National University, where I was awarded a First Class for my thesis.

I am enthusiastic about my work and enjoy engaging with the public to discuss science. I have given talks to audiences ranging from school children to academics, feel free to contact me to arrange a talk for your institution.
